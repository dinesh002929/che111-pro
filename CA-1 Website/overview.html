Overview

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Conventional vehicles emit CO2 and other greenhouse gases, contributing to global warming and climate change.

  • Air Pollution: Combustion engines release pollutants like nitrogen oxides (NOx) and particulate matter (PM), which degrade air quality and harm human health.

  • Fossil Fuel Dependency: Gasoline and diesel vehicles rely on non-renewable fossil fuels, leading to resource depletion.

  • Climate Change: The cumulative emissions from vehicles significantly accelerate climate change by increasing the concentration of GHGs in the atmosphere.

  • Health Risks: Vehicle emissions are linked to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular issues, and other health problems due to poor air quality.

  • Electric Vehicles (EVs):EVs reduce tailpipe emissions but may still have environmental impacts depending on how the electricity used for charging is generated.

  • Battery Production: EV batteries require rare metals, whose extraction and processing can harm ecosystems and communities.

  • Manufacturing Impact: The production of vehicles consumes large quantities of raw materials and energy, leading to environmental degradation.

  • Vehicle Disposal: Improper disposal of vehicles, especially batteries and hazardous materials, can contaminate soil and water.

  • Sustainable Alternatives: Public transportation, biking, and walking are greener alternatives that can significantly reduce the environmental footprint of transportation.
