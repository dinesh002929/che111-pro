Vehicle Comparison

Conventional Vehicles

  • Run on gasoline or diesel derived from fossil fuels.
  • Produce significant tailpipe emissions, including CO₂, NOx, and particulate matter.
  • Internal combustion engines have lower efficiency, converting only 20–30% of the fuel's energy into motion.
  • Subject to fluctuating gasoline/diesel prices, often higher on average.
  • Require frequent maintenance (oil changes, spark plugs, exhaust systems, etc.).
  • Typically offer longer driving ranges, up to 400-500 miles on a single tank of gas.

Electric Vehicles

  • Powered by electricity, which can come from various sources, including renewable energy.
  • Produce no tailpipe emissions. However, emissions depend on how the electricity is generated.
  • Electric motors are highly efficient, converting over 80% of the electrical energy into movement.
  • Electricity is generally cheaper, and renewable sources can lower the cost further.
  • Have fewer moving parts, requiring less maintenance (no oil changes, fewer repairs).
  • Average ranges are lower (200-300 miles), though some models can exceed 350 miles.
